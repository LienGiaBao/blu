Bac Lieu Prince's House: Timeless Majestic Architecture

C.E.P
November 1, 2024

If you're tired of the hustle and bustle of the city and want to escape to a peaceful place with unique cultural features, Bac Lieu is definitely the place for you. As a province in the Mekong Delta, Bac Lieu is known as a "land of milk and honey" with a fresh atmosphere and many outstanding tourist destinations.

When visiting here, you should add the Bac Lieu Prince's House to your itinerary. This place not only boasts a beautiful Western architectural style but also preserves artifacts and stories about the culture and life of the famous Bac Lieu prince.

1. General introduction to the Bac Lieu Prince's House

Located at 13 Dien Bien Phu Street, Ward 13, in the heart of Bac Lieu City, the Bac Lieu Prince's House stands out with its grandeur and timeless Western architectural style. It's also a cultural site associated with many stories and legends about the life of the most prominent young master in the Western region in the 1919s.

2. Exploring the history of the Bac Lieu Prince's House

This special house was built in 1919 when "Bac Lieu Prince" Tran Huy Trinh turned 19. The Bac Lieu Prince's House was designed and built by French engineers using high-quality materials imported from France. Due to its massive scale, locals called this famous house "Big House."

3. Experiencing a tour of the Bac Lieu Prince's House

As a tourist destination in Bac Lieu attracting a large number of visitors each year, the Bac Lieu Prince's House proves its immense appeal.

The unique architecture of the Bac Lieu Prince's House

The unique architecture of the Bac Lieu Prince's House has endured for over a century, thanks to careful preservation and restoration efforts. This architectural masterpiece, valued at an estimated 400 billion VND (about 16 million dollars), is a striking blend of Western elegance and traditional Eastern elements. Designed by French architects, the house features a dominant color palette of gold and white.

A glimpse into a corner of the Bac Lieu Prince's House.

Visitors to the house are often struck by its vast expanse, the exquisite interior design, and the array of precious artifacts housed within its aristocratic and opulent setting.

Every pillar is meticulously adorned with intricate and unique carvings. The entire house is bathed in a warm, inviting glow from soft yellow lights.

The ground floor features two bedrooms and a spacious hall, while a wide, gracefully curved staircase leads to the upper level.

The second floor comprises three bedrooms and two large, fully furnished halls. Above, a breezy balcony offers views of the river or market, making it the perfect spot to watch the sunrise or sunset.

The house exudes an air of elegance and nobility, adorned with numerous rare and valuable possessions collected by the Bac Lieu Prince.

Among the most striking features are a set of chairs and a table crafted from exquisite mother-of-pearl, and two yin-yang beds intricately carved with meticulous detail.

Visitors can also admire a collection of precious artifacts that reflect the prince's opulent lifestyle, including antique clocks, dragon-decorated teapots, rare vases, and an antique car purchased entirely in cash. These items serve as testament to the prince's lavish and opulent lifestyle.

The stories and legends surrounding the Bac Lieu Prince's House

The Bac Lieu Prince's House is steeped in a rich history and surrounded by numerous fascinating tales about the extravagant lifestyle of a renowned young man from the Mekong Delta over a century ago.

Tran Trinh Trach, the prince's father, was one of Saigon's most prominent tycoons, known for his business acumen and vast wealth. He spoiled his youngest son, the future Bac Lieu Prince, who would later become famous for his lavish lifestyle and daring exploits.

The Bac Lieu Prince, Tran Trinh Huy, was educated in France and even owned the first airplane in Vietnam. His life was filled with tales of opulence and extravagance, making him a legendary figure in the region. As the son of a wealthy tycoon, he was educated in France and had a passion for flying. Huy was the first person in Vietnam to own an airplane, which he used to survey his vast estates. His extravagant lifestyle and numerous accomplishments made him a legendary figure.

4. Planning a visit to the Bac Lieu Prince's House?

  • Here are a few things to keep in mind:
    • The house is open daily from 9 AM to 6 PM.
    • Admission fees are 30,000 VND for adults and 20,000 VND for children.
    • While guided tours are not always available, they can enhance your visit.
    • To experience the unique ambiance of staying in the prince's room, be sure to book your stay well in advance.
