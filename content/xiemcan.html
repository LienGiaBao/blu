Xiem Can Pagoda: One Of The Most Sparkling Pagodas In The South Of Viet Nam

C.E.P
November 1, 2024

Xiem Can pagoda is located in Vinh Trach Dong commune, Bac Lieu city, Bac Lieu province, and is considered the most ancient and sparkling pagoda in the Khmer temple system in the Southwest region. With sophisticated patterns and unique architecture, the pagoda, which is more than a century old, still majestically exists and welcomes thousands of vehicles to visit and worship.

The pagoda was built in 1887 and covers an area of about 5ha. Atfirst, Xiem Can pagoda had the Khmer name Komphisako, showing the erudition anddepth of Buddhist wisdom. The name Xiem Can of the pagoda comes from the Tieulanguage (the language of the people of Trieu Chau, China, currently living alot in Bac Lieu) which means "bordering the water" because theterrain of this land was formerly next to the alluvial plains, at the seaside.

The architectural complex of Xiem Can pagoda consists of manyitems facing the East, built according to the typical Theravada Buddhist schoolbut bearing the imprint of Angkor-Cambodia architecture.

The most prominent is the main hall of the temple. The main hallis built in the form of a rectangle, the main face facing east. The entrance tothe main hall with 18 steps to go up, above is a relief embossed BuddhaShakyamuni vivid and brilliant.

The walls, ceiling, and columns of the main hall are decoratedwith elaborate, colorful, and elaborate fresco reliefs. Outstanding large-sizedfrescoes tell the life of the Buddha from birth, to the process of cultivationto achieve righteous results.

Located on a 1.5m high brick foundation, the beauty inside themain hall has a vibrant, outstanding, and dignified color, with many delicatelysculpted round concrete columns creating solidity for the building.

The difference between Xiem Can Pagoda is the sophistication andmeticulousness in each motif, whether the walls, roof, pillars, etc. are allelaborately decorated and colorful. In addition, the pagoda has more than 100statues that are the incarnations of Shakyamuni Buddha, a stone stele carved in Ancient Khmer script, and a bronze bell dating from 1887. These works arehundreds of meters apart. In the middle are courtyards with rows of greentrees, the space exudes a calm and dignified look.

Not only is the temple famous for its unique architectural andartistic style, but Xiem Can Pagoda is also known as a place to preserve andform many cultural beauties in the spiritual life of the Khmer ethnic people.

For Khmer people, a pagoda is a place of community culturalactivities, preserving national identity, and worshiping the remains ofdeceased grandparents... Therefore, they are willing to put their efforts andwealth into the building and repairing them. pagoda. This is also the reasonwhy the temple is becoming more and more splendid and perfect.

Whats more special, this is also the place to keep ancient Khmerbooks written on the leaf up to 70 pages thick with an age of over 100 years.

Coming to Xiem Can Pagoda, you can not only admire the elaboratearchitecture, and enjoy the quiet and peaceful atmosphere, but also listen tothe Five Sound Orchestra - ethnic music of the Khmer people, the monkey-horseteam performed on major holidays)... understand more about the culture,beliefs, and life of the Khmer people in the Southern Delta.
